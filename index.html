“Should I keep trying?” he said. To himself. Because by now my attention was elsewhere. I mouthed along with him his next sentence, having already heard it three times this morning. This would be the fourth.

“Chances are that guy was her DJ fling, and that when I fell through, she followed up with him”.

Hindsight

This friend of mine, yesterday, had had this "sort of date thing" that he had been "really kinda looking forward to". It turned out that originally, somehow, (although strangely not out of character) he had misread their plan to “meet at 9” as “meet at 9 am”.

Painfully for him (and his circadian rhythm) he'd woken up at 6pm the evening before after a solid day's sleep. So when a night out with friends landed him home at 5am the day of the rendezvous he figured he'd stay up and spend the time getting ready, rather than attempt to sleep. Logical right?

At some point, around 8:15 or so, it dawned on him how how strange it would be for someone to finish work at 9am. Feeling somewhat like a monkey dancing simpleton, he re-read the sms with his newly found brain, and he now had 12 hours till the new "9".

Co-Tenency

Soon after, at about 9:15, his housemate informed him that he'd just given his 28 days notice to leave their share house. My friend spent the next 4 hours researching rent and bond law and tenancy transfers, and basically realised he was boned and was going to have to leave when his brother did, as they were under one tenancy agreement.

He spent the next 3 hours trying not to freak :P trying to work out how he could keep the place - and as he had to start re-getting ready to go out realised he'd already missed a late lunch with a friend he'd arranged, and a job search appointment he was meant to go to.

It was about this time, about 9 or so, that it really started to hit him that he'd been up about 30 hours :P but he really wanted to meet this girl out, it was why he hadn't slept the night before anyway, so he HAD to balls up!

Enthroned

When he turned up at the place his friend had invited him to, he came to realise that his friend had failed to inform him that what he was 'crashing' was a birthday party of someone my initial friend didn't know, and not only that, but that the other friend was trying to set him up with one of his co-workers … Somehow he managed to avoid social suicide at that venue, even if the cocktails were $17 each :) they did *look* amazing.

The whole time at the expensive venue wishing he was hanging out with the people he'd originally thought he was going to be at 9am :P torn that although uninvited he was obliged to now stay until his friend left. Damn obligation. At least the chairs were awesome. And they had pots for $8. And the conversation was actually keeping him awake quite well.

At 11:30 they left, and enroute he spent his last $2 on two coffees from 7 eleven, for him and his friend who he was hoping would reciprocate and shout a beer at the next venue :P

Enough

They rest, as the say, well … you had to be there.

It's possibly I over analysed this situation. That although not interested in the tall dude that … no … it was quite overt. I can't rationalise my way out of her change of attention to him when I didn't reciprocate.

I haven't been this … psychologically analytical and worried and intense regarding in a long time. I don't recalit with Mel. I remember it with Suzy. It's most noticeable feature is that I am driven to write down my thoughts. To prevent their cyclical nature.

With Mel it was more somewhat pathetically poetic.
