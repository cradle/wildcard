Hindsight

This friend of mine, yesterday, had had this “sort of date thing” that he had been “really kinda looking forward to”. He had gone on to inform me that somehow (although strangely not that out of character for him) he had misread their plan to “meet at 9” as “meet at 9 am”.

Painfully for him (and his circadian rhythm) he’d woken up at 6 pm the evening prior having had a solid day’s sleep. So when a night out with friends landed him home at 5am the day of the rendezvous, he figured he’d stay up and spend the time getting ready (rather than – say – attempt to sleep). Logical right?

He went on to explain that at some point, around 8:15am or so he thinks, it dawned on him how strange it would actually be for someone to finish work at 9am. Feeling somewhat the simpleton, he re-read the sms (with his newly found brain) and he now had 12 hours till the new “9”.

Co-Tenency

Not long after the “off by 12 hours” discovery (~9:15 by now) his housemate informed him that he’d just given his 28 days notice to leave their share house. My friend then spent the next 4 hours researching rent and bond law and tenancy transfers, and basically realised he was boned and was going to have to leave when his brother did, as they were under one tenancy agreement.

He spent the remaining few daylight hours not freaking out, rather trying to work out how he could keep the place. Researching leases, bonds, bond loans, private loans, gumtree, types of leases, co-tenancies, subleasing, tenancy acts, and multitudes of words that had always “just worked” until now.

Eventually, and all too soon, he had to start getting reading (again) for his 9 o’clock. As he only now checked his phone, it struck him he’d missed a late lunch with a friend, and a job search appointment he needed to attend.

It was about this time, about 9 or so, that it really started to hit him that he’d been up about 30 hours. The sleep deprivation and overwhelming madness of the last 24 hours was urging my friend to pike – to call the night off. But he really wanted to meet this girl again, it was why he hadn't slept the night before anyway, so he was going to turn up.

Enthroned

In anticipation of, and in parallel to, the evening, my friend arranged a last minute wingman of sorts in a mutual friend of mine and his. Our mutual friend informed him that he was initially to be out with a theatrical consort of his – and that as it was a at a public venue, my friend was welcome to accompany.

When he turned up at the place his friend had invited him to, he came to realise that his friend had failed to mention that he was unofficially “crashing” a birthday party of someone he didn't know, and not only that, but the other friend seemed to be trying to set him up with one of his co-workers …

Somehow (amidst the $17 cocktails, suits and high backed chairs) he did however manage to avoid birthday party suicide in spite of (or due to?) his eccentric conversations about wanting to own a thrown of his own design, as well as his musings on the social expectations of sexuality and how this relates to the notion of gender, polyamory and pansexuality. There was also a relatively mature conversation about mental health; which my friend found very refreshing.

Whilst at ‘Expensive Venue’ (wishing he was hanging out with the people he'd originally thought he was going to be at 9am) torn that although uninvited he was obliged to now stay until his friend left. Damn obligation. At least the chairs were awesome. And they had pots for $8. And the conversation was actually keeping him awake quite well.

At 11:30 they left, and enroute he spent his last $2 on two coffees from 7 eleven, for him and his friend who he was hoping would reciprocate and shout a beer at the next venue :P

Enough

They rest, as the say, well … you had to be there.

It's possibly I over analysed this situation. That although not interested in the tall dude that … no … it was quite overt. I can't rationalise my way out of her change of attention to him when I didn't reciprocate.

I haven't been this … psychologically analytical and worried and intense regarding in a long time. I don't recalit with Mel. I remember it with Suzy. It's most noticeable feature is that I am driven to write down my thoughts. To prevent their cyclical nature.

With Mel it was more somewhat pathetically poetic.
